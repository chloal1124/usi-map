Cost of Living and Disposable Income in Dallas

Urban Stress Index: 27.49 (Comfortable)

The cost of living in Dallas relative to income can be understood using the Urban Stress Index (USI). In Dallas, housing absorbs about 19.8% of a typical monthly income, while essential food costs account for roughly 7.7%. After rent and food, a typical earner retains around 72.5% of income for other living expenses, savings, and discretionary spending.

Methodology

The Urban Stress Index (USI) measures the share of a typical monthly income dispensed as essential housing and food costs for a single person with a local full time job. It combines two core components:

  • Housing burden – calculated as monthly rent for a one-bedroom apartment divided by typical monthly income.
  • Essential food share – calculated as the estimated cost of a standardized essential dining pattern divided by typical monthly income.

USI is defined as:
USI = Housing burden + Essential food share

All values are expressed as percentages of monthly income in local currency. The index is designed to provide a comparable indicator of cost-of-living pressure across cities, focusing on baseline expenses.

Cost Structure Analysis

Dallas has developed into one of the largest corporate and financial centres in the southern United States. Strong population growth and a diversified employment base in finance, technology, and logistics have supported steady income growth across the metropolitan region.

With housing costs remaining relatively moderate compared with coastal metropolitan areas, Dallas records a USI of roughly 27. This places the city among the more balanced large metropolitan economies in the United States.

Within the Texas urban system, Dallas forms an important economic corridor with nearby cities such as Austin and Houston. The city's affordability dynamics are often compared with fast-growing Sunbelt metropolitan areas such as Atlanta.

Sources

Income figures are based on nationally reported typical or median monthly earnings. Rental data reflects prevailing market rates for one-bedroom apartments within the metropolitan area.

Food costs are derived from standardized essential grocery basket estimates designed to represent a lifestyle of occasional dining-out in the local area.

All data are compiled from publicly available national statistics agencies, rental market listings, and aggregated consumer price datasets.
