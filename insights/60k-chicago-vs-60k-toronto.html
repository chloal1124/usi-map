60K in Toronto vs 60K in Chicago

What does a 60K starting salary actually look like once rent and food are accounted for?

Assume a recent graduate receives:

  • 60K CAD in Toronto
  • 60K USD in Chicago

On paper, both appear reasonable. The structural differences emerge after tax and housing.

After Tax Income

Assume approximate effective rates:

  • ~22.5% in Chicago
  • ~28% in Toronto

Estimated monthly take-home:

  • Chicago: ~3,875 USD
  • Toronto: ~3,600 CAD

The divergence does not begin here.

Rent

Assume a market-rate one-bedroom:

  • Chicago: ~1,606 USD
  • Toronto: ~2,222 CAD

Remaining after rent:

  • Chicago: ~2,269 USD
  • Toronto: ~1,378 CAD

Rent absorbs roughly:

  • ~40% of take-home in Chicago
  • Over 60% in Toronto

This is a structural gap.

Food

Assume baseline grocery + essential spending:

  • Chicago: ~500 USD
  • Toronto: ~630 CAD

Remaining after food:

  • Chicago: ~1,765 USD
  • Toronto: ~750 CAD

That remaining amount must still cover utilities, transit, insurance, savings, and unexpected expenses.

What 60K Feels Like

In Chicago at 60K, independent living remains feasible with room for savings.

In Toronto at 60K, financial flexibility narrows considerably.

The headline salary is identical. The economic breathing room is not.
